1. World Birth and Death Trend

This scene depicts the global trends of birth and death from 1950 to 2023.
You have the option to view it either in the normalized scale or in the absolute scale. If you wish to see it in the absolute scale please uncheck the checkbox below.

While both birth and death data show an overall upward trend, there are two significant spikes in the death data, indicating major calamities during the periods 1959 - 1961 and 2019 - 2021.
Feel free to move your mouse over the black circles to know more about the events. Let's now delve into these periods for a more detailed analysis.

2. Great Chinese Famine (1959 - 1961)

This scene shows the death trend for each region during 1955 - 1965.
You have the option to view it either in the normalized scale or in the absolute scale. If you wish to see it in the absolute scale please uncheck the checkbox below.

The Asia region seem to have been affected the most during this period. The other regions are relatively having a normal trend during this period.
Feel free to move your mouse over the black circles to know more about the events.

3. Covid 19 (2019 - Present)

This scene shows the death trend for each region during 2015 - 2023.
You have the option to view it either in the normalized scale or in the absolute scale. If you wish to see it in the absolute scale please uncheck the checkbox below.

All most all the regions have an increased death rate during the 2020 - 2021 period. Americas Region has the steepest increase in % of death from 2015, while Asia lost the most lives during this period.
Feel free to move your mouse over the black circles to know more about the events.

4. Playground!

This scene allows you to meddle around with the data.
You can choose the start year, end year and regions to see how the death rate correlates across different regions and years.
The start year and end year will act like a zoom to focus on the data points you are really interested in.
As always, you have the option to view it either in the normalized scale or in the absolute scale. If you wish to see it in the absolute scale please uncheck the checkbox below.
